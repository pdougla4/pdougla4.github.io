Senior Design — Red River UHS Operating Envelope in progress

I’m building a temperature-aware operating envelope for underground H₂ storage in the Red River A–C intervals: allowable (ΔT, rate q, BHP) combinations that satisfy three guardbands — AIG (caprock integrity under pressure + thermal stress), PGN (capillary sealing slack vs buoyancy), and TSI (thermal-shock risk). Outputs include P50/P90 confidence bands and a probability-of-compliance curve. A fast dual-porosity TH kernel drives UQ; a few tiny MINC and TH2M anchors validate exchange timing and stress coupling.

Show technical plan
  • Physics & outputs: Fast two-phase TH dual-porosity (Warren–Root + heat) for UQ; envelope of allowable (ΔT, q, BHP) with P50/P90 bands and compliance curve.
  • Guardbands: AIG (Biot + thermoelastic demand vs conservative microcrack/frac threshold), PGN (Pc50 vs Δρ g h with IFT(T,P,S), wettability), TSI (σth(ΔT)/σmicrocrack).
  • Anchors: MINC (3–4 shells) for matrix↔fracture exchange timing; small TH2M near-well/caprock micro-cases to calibrate partial-restraint R. Engines: OpenGeoSys; optional CMG cross-check.
  • Lab → model: Primary: Amott (Pc endpoints), low-field NMR T₂ (storativity split ω), contact angle (incl. anhydrite). Secondary: kerfed imbibition + He poro/perm (km, secondary φ), oriented plugs (kh, kv). Spacing L from slab photos → shape factor σ → exchange λ.
  • UQ: LHS → Morris → Sobol on trimmed drivers; bootstrapped quantiles. VoI recommends at most one next test if it materially shrinks P90–P50.
  • Reproducibility: repo with Docker/CI, runs.csv, commands.txt, and a one-page Datasheet (PDF + JSON).
